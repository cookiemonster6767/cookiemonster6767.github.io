Question 1 of 10 - Essential Supplies
What is the most important item you need BEFORE bringing a cat home?
Correct Answer: B
A litter box is essential from day one! Cats are naturally clean animals and need a proper place to go to the toilet. Food, water bowls, and the litter box are the absolute essentials before your cat arrives.
Question 2 of 10 - Cat Myths
True or False: Cats always land on their feet.
Correct Answer: FALSE
This is a common myth! While cats have an amazing "righting reflex" that helps them turn mid-air, they don't ALWAYS land on their feet. Falls from low heights or unexpected tumbles can result in injuries. Never assume your cat will be safe from a fall!
Question 3 of 10 - Body Language
What does it mean when a cat's tail is straight up in the air?
Correct Answer: B
A tail held high is a sign of a happy, confident cat! It's like a friendly wave. They often do this when greeting their owners or other cats they like. A puffed-up tail or a tail tucked down indicates fear or aggression.
Question 4 of 10 - Cat Health
How often should you take a healthy adult cat to the vet?
Correct Answer: B
Healthy adult cats should visit the vet at least once a year for a check-up and vaccinations. Senior cats (over 7 years) should go twice a year. Regular vet visits help catch health problems early!
Question 5 of 10 - Cat Behavior
True or False: When a cat brings you a dead mouse or bird, it's trying to feed you because it thinks you're a bad hunter.
Correct Answer: TRUE
Believe it or not, your cat is trying to help! Cats are natural hunters, and when they bring you prey, they're either trying to teach you to hunt (like they would with kittens) or sharing their catch with their family. It's actually a sign of affection!
Question 6 of 10 - Essential Supplies
Why is a scratching post important for cats?
Correct Answer: C
Scratching is natural cat behavior! It helps them remove the dead outer layer of their claws, stretch their muscles, and mark their territory with scent glands in their paws. Without a scratching post, they'll scratch your furniture instead!
Question 7 of 10 - Body Language
What does purring usually mean?
Correct Answer: A
Purring usually means your cat is happy, content, and relaxed. However, cats can also purr when stressed or in pain as a self-soothing behavior. Pay attention to their other body language signals too!
Question 8 of 10 - Cat Myths
True or False: Cats can drink milk with no problems.
Correct Answer: FALSE
This is a big myth! Most adult cats are actually lactose intolerant. Drinking milk can give them an upset stomach and diarrhea. Water is the best drink for cats. If you want to give them a treat, use special cat milk from pet shops.
Question 9 of 10 - Cat Behavior
Why do cats knead (push their paws in and out) on soft surfaces?
Correct Answer: B
Kneading is adorable and comes from kittenhood! Kittens knead their mother's belly to stimulate milk flow while nursing. Adult cats continue this behavior when they feel safe, happy, and comfortable. It's a huge compliment if your cat kneads on you!
Question 10 of 10 - Cat Health
Which of these foods is toxic to cats and should NEVER be given to them?
Correct Answer: B
Chocolate is extremely toxic to cats (and dogs)! It contains theobromine, which cats cannot digest properly. Other toxic foods include onions, garlic, grapes, raisins, and anything with xylitol (artificial sweetener). Always stick to proper cat food!
