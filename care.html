Essential Cat Care Information

Before bringing your cat home, make sure you have these essential supplies:

  • Food & Water Bowls: Stainless steel or ceramic bowls work best
  • Cat Food: High-quality cat food appropriate for their age
  • Litter Box & Litter: One per cat, plus one extra
  • Cat Bed: A cosy, warm place to sleep
  • Scratching Post: Essential to protect your furniture!
  • Toys: Balls, mice, feather wands for playtime
  • Grooming Brush: For keeping their coat healthy
  • Carrier: For vet visits and travel
  • Collar & ID Tag: With your contact information

â¤ï¸ How to Treat Your New Cat

The First Few Days:

  • Be Patient: Your cat will be nervous in their new home - this is completely normal!
  • Quiet Space: Give them a quiet room to settle in first with their litter box, food, and bed
  • Let Them Explore: Don't force interaction - let them come to you when ready
  • Speak Softly: Use a calm, gentle voice around them
  • Routine: Feed them at the same times each day to help them feel secure
  • Play Time: Use toys to build trust and bond with them
  • Hiding is Normal: They may hide for a few days - don't worry!

Remember: It can take 2-3 weeks for a cat to fully settle into a new home.

ğŸ½ï¸ Food Portions by Cat Age

Kittens (2-12 months):

  • 3-4 small meals per day
  • Approximately 200-250 calories per day
  • Use kitten-specific food (higher protein & calories)

Adult Cats (1-7 years):

  • 2 meals per day
  • Approximately 200-300 calories per day (depending on size)
  • Average 4kg cat needs about 180-200g wet food daily

Senior Cats (7+ years):

  • 2-3 smaller meals per day
  • Approximately 180-200 calories per day
  • May need senior-specific food with joint support

Note: Always provide fresh water and adjust portions based on your cat's activity level and weight. Consult your vet for specific recommendations!
